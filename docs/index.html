Who it's for

gaming

Party chat

Mechanical keyboards are loud, and your squad hears every key. Prism cleans your mic for Discord and in-game voice, and because it runs on CPU it doesn't take frames from your game.

calls & work

Meetings from real rooms

Fans, generators, traffic, a busy household. You don't need a quiet room to sound clear on Zoom or Teams, and it works the same in every meeting app at once.

streaming

Streams without an RTX

Noise removal like NVIDIA Broadcast, without needing an NVIDIA card. Prism shows up in OBS as a regular microphone and runs on any machine.

What it does

live now

AI noise removal

Removes fan noise, keyboard clatter, and background hum while you talk, not just between words. Two models to pick from: RNNoise (very light) or DeepFilterNet3 (stronger). You can switch them live and set how aggressive the cleanup is.

phase 3 · next

Voice isolation

Background voices are harder than background noise. The goal here is separating your voice from roommates, the TV, or music in the room, so only you end up in the call.

How it works

  1. 01

    Install

    Install VB-Audio Virtual Cable once, then run Prism.

  2. 02

    Pick the mic

    In Discord, Zoom, OBS, or any app, choose CABLE Output as your microphone.

  3. 03

    Talk

    That's it. Noise removal is on by default. Switch models or adjust the strength while you talk if you want to.

What you need

  • Windows 10 or 11 (Linux and macOS are planned)
  • VB-Audio Virtual Cable, free, installed once (run as admin, reboot)
  • Python 3.11+ for now; a packaged .exe is on the roadmap
